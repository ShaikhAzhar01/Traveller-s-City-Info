Welcome to Jammu!

Jammu, also known as the "City of Temples," is the winter capital of the Indian union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. Situated on the banks of the Tawi River, Jammu is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, historical monuments, and religious significance. It serves as a gateway to the popular pilgrimage destinations of Vaishno Devi and Amarnath.

Besides its religious significance, Jammu is also famous for its scenic beauty, with picturesque landscapes, lush greenery, and stunning mountains. The city offers a blend of tradition and modernity, making it a captivating destination for tourists.

Brief Information

  • Raghunath Temple

    • Location: City Chowk, Jammu
    • Timings: 5:00 am to 12:00 pm, 4:00 pm to 9:00 pm; every day
    • Entry fee: Free
    • Must-see: Magnificent architecture, intricate carvings, and sacred shrines dedicated to various Hindu deities
    • Special Events: Navratri and Ram Navami celebrations

  • Bahu Fort

    • Location: Bahu Wali Mata Road, Jammu
    • Timings: 6:00 am to 8:00 pm; every day
    • Entry fee: Free
    • Must-see: Ancient fortification, panoramic views of Tawi River and surrounding landscapes
    • Facilities: Guided tours, historical insights, and scenic photography spots

  • Mubarak Mandi Palace

    • Location: Mubarak Mandi Complex, Jammu
    • Timings: 10:00 am to 5:00 pm; closed on Mondays
    • Entry fee: INR 20 for adults, INR 10 for children
    • Must-see: Architectural marvel, blend of Rajasthani, Mughal, and Gothic styles, intricate artwork, and historical artifacts
    • Special Events: Heritage walks, cultural exhibitions, and light and sound shows

  • Amar Mahal Palace Museum

    • Location: Palace Road, Jammu
    • Timings: 9:00 am to 5:00 pm; every day
    • Entry fee: INR 50 for adults, INR 25 for children
    • Must-see: Opulent palace architecture, royal artifacts, library, and art collection
    • Special Events: Exhibitions, cultural performances, and educational programs

  • Bagh-e-Bahu Aquarium

    • Location: Bahu Fort, Jammu
    • Timings: 9:00 am to 6:00 pm; every day
    • Entry fee: INR 40 for adults, INR 20 for children
    • Must-see: Diverse marine life, underwater habitats, and interactive displays
    • Facilities: Educational tours, audiovisual presentations, and souvenir shop
